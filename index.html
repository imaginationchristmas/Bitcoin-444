The Fundamentals

Why Bitcoin is Different

Here are some big fun facts about BTC that should push anyone towards Bitcoin. Feel free to dive deeper and really take the orange pill.

₿
100 Million Satoshis

1 Bitcoin = 100 million Satoshis (Sats). Think of it like pennies in a dollar — you'll mostly interact with Bitcoin in fractional Sat form.

📦
Fixed Supply: 21 Million

Only 21 million Bitcoin will ever exist. Hard-coded into the protocol. No one can print more — not governments, not Satoshi, not anyone.

🔗
Peer-to-Peer

Send money directly to anyone on earth without a bank, payment processor, or government in the middle. It's your money — no approval needed.

🏅
Better Than Gold

Bitcoin has all the properties of gold — scarce, durable, divisible — but it's also portable, verifiable, and can be sent across the world in minutes.

🌐
Decentralized

No single company, country, or person controls Bitcoin. It runs on thousands of nodes worldwide. There's no CEO to call, no server to shut down.

📈
4-Year Track Record

Pick any date in Bitcoin's history. Come back 4 years later. The price has been higher every single time. No other asset has that track record.

📉
Everything Gets Cheaper in BTC

A house that cost 1,000 BTC in 2011 costs a fraction of a Bitcoin today. Priced in Bitcoin, everything gets cheaper over time. That's the opposite of inflation.

🌍
No Borders

Send Bitcoin to anyone, anywhere, any time — 24/7/365. No wire fees, no business hours, no "your transaction is under review." It just works.

Step 3

Take Custody of Your Bitcoin

Now in order to actually own your Bitcoin you need to be in control of those keys. It’s the same for anything financial related. Remember the Canadian truckers?

🔑

Seed Phrase Knowledge

Whenever you start/generate a new wallet, it will give you a 12 or 24 word seed phrase. The wallet generates it randomly and is only shown fully once. This is essentially your Bitcoin. If someone gets access to your seed phrase, they can wipe your entire wallet and be gone forever.

✍️
Write It Down

Take your time. Write all words in order, spelled correctly. Store it in a weather-protected home safe. Laminating it adds another layer of protection.

🔒
Never Digital

Do NOT take a photo of it, type it into any app, or store it anywhere online. Ever. If it touches the internet, assume it's compromised.

🤐
Tell Nobody

Your seed phrase is more sacred than your SSN. Don't share it with anyone — ever. And don't tell people how much Bitcoin you have. Not even family.

🔨
Stamp It in Metal

Stamp your seed phrase into a metal plate for fire and flood resistance. stampseed.com ↗ is a solid option.

💡 Pro Tip When setting up a new wallet: write down your seed phrase → wipe the wallet → restore it with your seed phrase → confirm it works → THEN add Bitcoin. This verifies you wrote it down properly before any funds are at risk.

There’s stories of people who got into Bitcoin in the pioneer days and lost the seed phrase. Don’t be a cheapass or dumbass and lose access to generational wealth.

Start off with a little bit when sending so you get the hang of it. Eventually you’ll build up the confidence and knowledge to start slinging BTC like a MF BOSS.

Look into UTXO management only when you get the basics down.

Wallet Types

Choosing Your Wallet

There are two types of wallets — a hot wallet and a cold wallet. Where you store depends on how much you have and how long you plan to hold.

A hot wallet is connected to the internet — convenient for quick access and small daily transactions (similar to Venmo, Cash App, PayPal). A hot wallet is a good stepping stone if you are not ready to make the leap to a cold storage wallet.

Strike

Built-in Lightning wallet — great for small, fast payments. Also where you buy sats.

Blue Wallet

Open-source, Bitcoin and Lightning support. Simple interface, widely respected.

Muun Wallet

Beginner-friendly wallet with seamless Lightning support. Easy to get started.

Aqua Wallet

Mobile-first option designed for ease of use with Lightning and on-chain support.

A cold storage wallet is completely offline — the modern day’s version of a gold vault. Since it’s not online it’s almost impossible for hackers to get into it. Not for everyday use — act like it’s your life savings.

Rules of the Road

Do's & Don'ts

You’re gonna have a ton of questions after this. Not all of this will make sense yet — that is normal.

✓ Do This

  • Get some and educate yourself and your inner circle
  • DCA — don’t try to time the market
  • Take self custody of your Bitcoin
  • Start small when learning to send BTC
  • Spread holdings across multiple wallets
  • Learn about the $5 wrench attack and practice security
  • HODL — hold long term

✗ Don't Do This

  • Talk about how much Bitcoin you have
  • Share your seed phrase with ANYONE
  • Try to trade in and out for quick profits
  • Photograph or digitally store your seed phrase
  • Buy other cryptocurrencies (they're mostly scams)
  • Sell your Bitcoin — institutional money is buying, not selling
  • Put a target on your back bragging about your stack

The 4-Year Rule

"I'm going to buy at this price and sell it when I make a big enough profit" — don't. You'll pay capital gains taxes and you'll probably sell too early. There has never been a point in Bitcoin's history that it has not been higher 4 years later.

"It takes 4 years to see significant gains and 8 years to make life changing money."

Remember not everyone will get in at this price. Envy, jealousy, greed, and evil will rise with Bitcoin’s adoption, unfortunately. DON’T put a target on your back about any of this. Be smart with every move and intention.

#1 Rule of BTC: don’t talk about how much you have (seed phrase security is #1 too).
