## Step 3: Take Custody of Your Bitcoin 


Now in order to actually own your Bitcoin you need to be in control of those keys. To make it simple for you, you need to get your Bitcoin off the exchanges and into your own wallet. When it’s on the exchange you don’t actually own that Bitcoin. 
It’s the same for anything financial related. Remember the Canadian truckers? 


There’s two types of wallets - a hot wallet and a cold wallet. But first let’s go over the most important aspect of any of your wallets - your seed phrase 


### Seed Phrase Knowledge 

Whenever you start/generate any new wallet, it will give you instructions on setting it up and provide you with a 12 or 24 word seed phrase. The wallet generates it randomly and is only shown fully once. It is critical you take your time and write them down in order and spelled right 

It’s a randomized list of 2048 words that gets generated out of random. 
When you set up a wallet, especially BTC only hardware wallets with a camera, there’s security measures to know that the device wasn’t tampered with  

This and actually sending a transaction is scary at first. Trust me everyone has those first time jitters when doing these. Repetition and knowledge are key to getting over the hump and actually taking control over your BTC 

Start off with a little bit when sending so you get the hang of it. Eventually you’ll build up the confidence and knowledge to start slinging BTC like a MF BOSS  

This is essentially your Bitcoin 


You could delete the app or physically destroy your hardware device and still access your Bitcoin via seed phrase. 

You don’t share your seed phrase with anyone; it’s more sacred than your Social Security number.  

If someone gets access to your seed phrase, they can wipe your entire wallet and be gone forever 

DO NOT take a picture of it or store it anywhere online 


Temporarily, write it down very clean and legible and store it safely in a weather protected home safe. Getting it laminated is another step in the right direction. 

You’ll need your seed phrase in order to access and send your Bitcoin. There’s stories of people who got into Bitcoin in the pioneer days and lost the seed phrase 

Don’t be a cheapass or dumbass and lose access to generational wealth

### Multi Sig Wallets 

Multi sig wallets are pretty much like a Bitcoin vault that needs more than one key to open it. You’d need 2 of 3 keys to sign off a transaction  

More secure - it takes multiple approvals instead of just one person  

Shared control - businesses, families, groups/organizations can hold a key  

It’s even better than a traditional hardware wallet because it protects you in case you lose your seed phrase or are physically attacked for your seed phrase. There’s also an inheritance plan in place too  

Since it is a service, there will be a cost involved to have a multi sig. Some of the better multi sig companies require you to have $50,000 in BTC just to open the account
